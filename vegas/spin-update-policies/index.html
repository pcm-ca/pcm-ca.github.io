  • cone30 and cone15

    Also known as small step moves, the cone30 and cone15 moves generates new spin directions from a uniform probability distribution within a cone with a given opening angle around the initial spin direction, hence each spin can only move by small angular changes limited by the opening angle. The openin angle can be set to 303030Âº (cone30) or 151515Âº (cone15). These trial moves make use of the Rodriguesâ€™ rotation formula.

  • hn30 and hn15

    hn30 and hn15 are based on a trial move proposed by D. Hinzke and U. Nowak that performs a combined samplig. For each Monte Carlo step one trial move is randomly selected from a set of three random, one small step and one spin flip moves. Then, the selected trial move is applied to all the spin moments in that Monte Carlo step. hn30 and hn15 correspond to trial moves where the set is composed by a cone30 and cone15 moves, respectively.
